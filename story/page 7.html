Two o'clock, sang a voice.

Delicately sensing decay at last,
the regiments of mice hummed out as softly
as blown grayleaves in an electrical wind.

Two-fifteen.

The dog was gone.
In the cellar, the incinerator glowed suddenly
and a whirl of sparks leaped up the chimney.

Two thirty-five.

Bridge tables sprouted from patio walls.
Playing cards fluttered onto pads in a shower of pips.
Martinis manifested on an oaken bench with
egg-salad sandwiches. Music played.
But the tables were silent and the cards untouched.

At four o'clock the tables folded like great
butterflies back through the paneled walls.

Revisualized by Virginia deGuzman
