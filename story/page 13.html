Ten more voices died. In the last instant under the fire avalanche, other choruses,oblivious, could be heard announcing the time, playing music, cutting the lawn by remote-controlmower, or setting an umbrella frantically out and in the slamming and opening front door, athousand things happening, like a clock shop when each clock strikes the hour insanely before orafter the other, a scene of maniac confusion, yet unity; singing, screaming, a few last cleaning mice darting bravely out to carry the horrid ashes away!

And one voice, with sublime disregard for the situation, read poetry aloud in the fiery study, until all the film spools burned, until all thewires withered and the circuits cracked.
